# Analytics Dashboard

## What it does

AquaFlow's analytics dashboard gives an Owner a real-time view of how their
business is actually performing — without any manual report-building.

Typical visibility the dashboard provides:

- Total deliveries completed (daily/weekly/monthly)
- Revenue collected over a given period
- Outstanding balances across all customers
- Top customers by volume or revenue
- Driver performance (deliveries completed per driver)
- Bottle balances outstanding across the business

## Why it matters

Water suppliers running on paper or Excel typically have **zero real
visibility** into their own business beyond gut feeling. Questions like
"Are we growing?", "Which customers matter most?", "How much do we
currently have outstanding in unpaid balances?" are essentially unanswerable
without hours of manual work — if they're answerable at all.

AquaFlow's dashboard turns these into an instant, always-up-to-date answer,
because the underlying data is already being captured as part of normal
day-to-day delivery and payment operations — nothing extra to enter.

## Who uses it

The analytics dashboard is part of the Owner role. It's meant to support
business decisions — where to focus collection efforts, which customers are
most valuable, whether delivery volume is trending up or down — not just to
look impressive.

## Why this matters for growing suppliers

The bigger a supplier's operation gets, the more this visibility matters.
A small operation might get by on gut feel; a supplier doing 20+ deliveries
a day across many customers and drivers genuinely cannot track business
performance accurately without a system like this.
