# Monthly Subscriptions

## What it does

Most water delivery customers order on a recurring basis — the same
quantity of bottles, on a regular schedule, month after month. AquaFlow lets
suppliers set up each customer's recurring plan once, and then generates
their deliveries automatically going forward.

A subscription defines:

- The customer
- How many bottles per delivery
- Delivery frequency (e.g. daily, every 2 days, weekly)
- The monthly rate/billing arrangement for that customer

## Why it matters

Without subscriptions built into the system, an Owner (or whoever manages
deliveries) has to manually remember or re-write every recurring customer's
order, every single day. This is exactly the kind of repetitive manual work
that eats hours of a business owner's week and is highly error-prone —
easy to forget a customer, or get the quantity wrong.

With AquaFlow, once a customer's subscription is set up, their deliveries
appear automatically in the daily delivery schedule with zero manual
re-entry, freeing up the owner's time for actually running the business.

## Changing or pausing a subscription

Subscriptions can be updated any time a customer's needs change (more/fewer
bottles, different frequency) or paused/cancelled if a customer stops their
service — keeping the delivery schedule accurate without manual cleanup.

## Subscriptions and billing

Subscription customers can be billed monthly based on their plan, tying
directly into Payment Management and Outstanding Balance Tracking (see
`09-payments.md`) so the Owner always knows who's paid for the month and
who hasn't.
