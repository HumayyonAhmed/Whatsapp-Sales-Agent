# Bottle Tracking

## What it does

AquaFlow tracks exactly how many bottles are with each customer at any given
time — bottles delivered, bottles collected (empties returned), and the net
balance outstanding per customer.

## Why it matters

Bottles are a real, physical asset, and in a paper-based or Excel-based
business it's extremely common to lose track of exactly how many bottles
are out with customers. This causes two costly problems:

1. **Bottle shrinkage** — bottles that are delivered but never tracked as
   "owed back" simply disappear over time, and the business eats the cost
   of replacing them.
2. **Customer disputes** — without a record, disagreements over "how many
   bottles do I actually have" are common and hard to resolve fairly.

## How it works

Every delivery records exactly how many bottles were dropped off and how
many empties were picked up. AquaFlow keeps a running per-customer bottle
balance automatically — so at any moment, an Owner (or a driver on their
route) can see exactly how many bottles are outstanding with any given
customer.

## Why this matters more as a business grows

For a very small operation, bottle counts might be manageable by memory.
Once a supplier crosses into 20+ deliveries a day across many customers,
manual bottle tracking becomes practically impossible to do accurately —
this is one of the clearest, most concrete ways AquaFlow pays for itself:
recovering bottles (or their cost) that would otherwise quietly go missing.
